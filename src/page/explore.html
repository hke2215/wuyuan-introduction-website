Ancient Village Tour

Three Must-Visit Ancient Villages

Every ancient village is a living history, and every alleyway holds a thousand-year-old story.

🔥 Most popular

Huangling Ancient Village

📍 Northeast of Wuyuan County ⏱ Recommended visit time: 4-5 hours

The ancient village, perched on a mountain cliff, is renowned for its unique "autumn drying" agricultural custom. Hundreds of Hui-style ancient houses are arranged in layers along the mountainside. Every autumn, villagers dry their harvested peppers, chrysanthemums, pumpkins, and other produce on drying trays on their rooftops, creating a colorful harvest scene.

Autumn drying spectacle Tianjie Ancient Alley Terraced fields and sea of ​​flowers Ancient and famous trees
💡 Travel Tips: Autumn (September-November) is the best time to view the autumn harvest. It is recommended to arrive early in the morning to avoid crowds and enjoy the best panoramic view of the autumn harvest from a high vantage point.

Likeng Ancient Village

📍 Qiukou Town, Wuyuan County ⏱ Recommended visit time: 3-4 hours

This ancient village, predominantly inhabited by people with the surname Li, perfectly embodies the quintessential Jiangnan water town scene of small bridges, flowing water, and traditional houses. Ancient buildings from the Ming and Qing dynasties are scattered throughout the village, with streams flowing through it and houses built along the banks, spanned by stone arch bridges, creating a natural ink painting.

Small bridge and flowing water Ming and Qing Dynasty ancient buildings Shenming Pavilion A paradise for sketching
💡 Travel Tips: Likeng has a unique charm on a rainy day. It is recommended to bring an umbrella and stroll through the ancient alleys to experience the ink-wash painting-like atmosphere in the misty rain.
⭐ 5A scenic area

Jiangwan Ancient Town

📍 Jiangwan Town, Wuyuan County ⏱ Recommended visit time: 3-4 hours

A national 5A-level tourist attraction, this ancient town boasts a history of over a thousand years. It is one of the birthplaces of Wuyuan culture, preserving a well-maintained Hui-style architectural complex, including the Xiaojiang Ancestral Hall, Yongsi Street, Teng Family Old House, and many other historical sites, making it an excellent window into understanding Huizhou culture.

Xiaojiang Ancestral Hall Yongsi Street Huizhou-style three carvings Celebrity hometown
💡 Travel Tips: You can ask a local guide to explain the cultural codes hidden in the architectural details; every carving has a profound meaning.
Architectural Art

Hui-style architecture: A poem frozen in time

The white walls, black tiles, and horse-head gables are not merely architectural features, but also a three-dimensional representation of Huizhou merchant culture, clan system, and feng shui philosophy.

🏯

Horse-head wall

The staggered, stepped gables, resembling a proud horse, serve as both firewalls and symbols of status. The progressively advancing contours outline the most iconic skyline of Hui-style architecture.

🪵

Three-carving art

The brick carvings are exquisitely detailed, the stone carvings are robust and imposing, and the wood carvings are delicate and translucent. From the gate tower to the window lattice, from the beams and pillars to the brackets, every part is a masterpiece of art created with ingenuity.

🏛️

Ancestral Hall Culture

The ancestral hall is the spiritual center of the ancient village, carrying the glory and memories of the family. The tall gate tower, the exquisite stage, and the solemn ancestral hall bear witness to the rise and fall of the Huizhou clan system.

💧

Feng Shui Pattern

Nestled against mountains and facing water, sheltered from the wind and bathed in sunlight, the ancient villages of Wuyuan are all situated in accordance with traditional Feng Shui principles. From the waterside gardens to the moon-shaped ponds, every layout embodies the wisdom of our ancestors.

Folk Experience

Living cultural heritage

01

Huizhou Opera Performance

One of the predecessors of Peking Opera, this singing style, which has been performed on the ancient stage of Wuyuan for hundreds of years, is a national intangible cultural heritage. Its soaring and passionate melodies and exquisite costumes will take you on a journey through time.

🎭 Intangible cultural heritage
02

Wuyuan Tea Ceremony

Wuyuan green tea is described as having a naturally vibrant green color and a rich, fragrant flavor. Visitors can stroll through tea gardens to pick tea leaves, learn the art of tea making, and appreciate the timeless charm of tea culture, experiencing its profound depth and breadth.

🍵 Millennium Tea Country
03

Nuo dance for blessings

Known as a "living fossil of Chinese dance," Wuyuan Nuo dance uses simple masks, mysterious rituals, and bold dance steps to recreate the beliefs and wishes of our ancient ancestors.

💃 Living fossil
04

Huizhou ink production

Experience the thousand-year-old craft of Huizhou ink making by grinding pine soot by hand. A good ink stick is "light to the touch, clear when ground, and fragrant when smelled," embodying the spiritual pursuit of Huizhou literati.
